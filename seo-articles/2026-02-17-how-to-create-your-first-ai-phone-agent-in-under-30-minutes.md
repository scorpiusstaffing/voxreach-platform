## Minute 6–15: Design Your Call Flow (The Brain)

Now we tell the AI what to say and do. VoxReach uses a visual conversation designer—think of it as a flowchart for phone calls.

1. **Start with the greeting.**  
   You’ll see a block labeled “Greeting.” Edit the text to something like:  
   *“Hi, thanks for calling [Your Business Name]. This is an AI assistant. How can I help you today?”*

2. **Add intent detection.**  
   The next block asks, “What should the AI listen for?” Add 3–4 options:
   - *“I’d like to book an appointment.”*
   - *“I have a question about your services.”*
   - *“I need to speak with someone.”*
   - *“I’m a current customer.”*

3. **Map each intent to an action.**  
   - For “book an appointment,” connect it to a **scheduler block**.  
   - For “question about services,” connect it to a **knowledge‑base block**.  
   - For “speak with someone,” connect it to a **transfer block** (to your cell or office line).  
   - For “current customer,” connect it to a **special handling block** (maybe a different transfer).

4. **Set up the scheduler.**  
   If you use Google Calendar or Outlook, connect it now (VoxReach has one‑click integrations). If not, the AI can collect details and email them to you.

5. **Add a fallback.**  
   There’s always a block called “Didn’t understand.” Set it to: *“I’m sorry, I didn’t catch that. Could you repeat, or say ‘agent’ to speak with a person?”*

**Pro tip:** Keep it simple. Your first agent only needs to handle 80% of calls—the routine stuff. Complex issues can go to a human.

**Time check:** 15 minutes in, you have a complete call flow. The AI knows what to do for each common request.

## Minute 16–22: Train the Knowledge Base (The Memory)

Your AI needs to know about your business. This is where you upload FAQs.

1. **Click “Knowledge Base” in the left menu.**
2. **Click “Add Document.”**
3. **Paste your FAQ questions and answers.** Use this format:

   ```
   Q: What are your hours?
   A: We’re open Monday–Friday, 9 AM–5 PM, and Saturday 10 AM–2 PM.

   Q: Do you offer free consultations?
   A: Yes, we offer a 15‑minute free consultation for new clients.

   Q: Where are you located?
   A: Our office is at 123 Main Street, but we serve clients nationwide via phone.

   Q: How much does [service] cost?
   A: Pricing starts at $99/month. Would you like me to connect you with a sales rep for a custom quote?
   ```

   You can also upload a PDF of your service menu or a webpage URL—VoxReach will extract the text automatically.

4. **Save.** The AI indexes this content instantly.

**Why this works:** When a caller asks, “What are your hours?” the AI searches the knowledge base and answers in a natural voice, using the exact wording you provided.

**Time check:** 22 minutes in, your AI is now informed about your business.

## Minute 23–27: Choose Your Voice and Test (The Personality)

The voice is the personality of your agent. Pick one that fits your brand.

1. **Click “Voice & Settings.”**
2. **Listen to the voice samples.** You’ll hear male/female options with different accents and tones.  
   - **Professional services** (law, finance): pick a calm, authoritative voice.  
   - **Health/wellness:** pick a warm, empathetic voice.  
   - **Sales/retail:** pick an energetic, friendly voice.
3. **Select your favorite.** You can adjust speech rate (slow it down 10% for clarity).
4. **Test the agent.**  
   Click the “Test Call” button. VoxReach will call your mobile number. Answer and run through the scenarios:
   - Ask for hours.
   - Try to book an appointment.
   - Ask a random question (see if it uses the knowledge base).
   - Request a human.

   Listen to the flow—does it feel natural? If something sounds off, go back and tweak the script.

**Time check:** 27 minutes in, you’ve tested the agent and it’s ready for real calls.

2. **Add the number to your website, Google Business Profile, and email signature.**

## Common 30‑Minute Mistakes (And Quick Fixes)

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| **Voice too fast** | Slow speech rate to 90% in voice settings. |
| **FAQ answers too long** | Trim answers to 1–2 sentences. Callers don’t want a monologue. |
| **No fallback** | Ensure the “Didn’t understand” block offers a human transfer. |
| **Calendar not connected** | Use the “collect info” option and have the AI email you the details. |
| **Testing with poor cell service** | Use a landline or VoIP app for clearer test audio. |
