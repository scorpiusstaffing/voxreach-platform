## Step 3: Set Up Your Phone Number (Cost‑Effective Options)

Your AI agent needs a phone number to make or receive calls. As a small business, you have three affordable paths:

1. **Get a new local number from your AI platform** (recommended).  
   Platforms like VoxReach include local or toll‑free numbers for no extra monthly fee. You pick the area code, and the number is ready in seconds.

2. **Port your existing business number** (if you have strong brand recognition).  
   Porting usually takes 3–7 business days and may involve a small fee, but it lets you keep the number customers already know.

   Keep your existing carrier and set up forward‑on‑busy or forward‑always to your AI agent’s number. This avoids porting delays but can slightly reduce audio quality.

**Budget‑conscious advice:** Start with a new number from your AI platform. It’s instant, free, and you can always port your main number later once you’re confident in the system.

## Step 4: Create Scripts That Sound Authentic (Not Robotic)

The script is the heart of your AI voice agent. A great script sounds like a friendly, helpful person—not a machine reading a list of prompts.

### Small‑Business Script Template (Inbound Call Example)

**Use case:** A local plumbing company that wants to answer after‑hours emergency calls.

> **Agent:** “Hi, this is [Company Name] plumbing. You’ve reached our after‑hours line. Are you calling about a plumbing emergency?”  
> **Caller:** “Yes, my kitchen sink is overflowing.”  
> **Agent:** “I’m sorry to hear that. We can send a technician out right away. To get you on the schedule, I need to ask a few quick questions. First, is the water still running, or have you shut it off?”  
> **Caller:** “I shut it off under the sink.”  
> **Agent:** “Great. Next, what’s your street address so we can dispatch the nearest technician?”  
> *(Caller provides address)*  
> **Agent:** “Thanks. Our emergency dispatch fee is $99, and the technician will give you a quote for the repair when they arrive. Does that work for you?”  
> **Caller:** “Yes, that’s fine.”  
> **Agent:** “Perfect. I’ve scheduled a technician to arrive within the next 90 minutes. You’ll receive a text with their name and ETA shortly. Is there anything else I can help with tonight?”  
> **Caller:** “No, that’s all.”  
> **Agent:** “Okay. Stay safe, and we’ll see you soon.”

**Why this script works for small businesses:**
- **Empathetic tone** – Acknowledges the caller’s stress.
- **Clear next steps** – Guides the conversation toward scheduling.
- **Transparent pricing** – Mentions the dispatch fee upfront.
- **Reassurance** – Provides ETA and follow‑up text.

**Script‑writing checklist for SMBs:**
- [ ] Use short, conversational sentences.
- [ ] Include your business name in the greeting.
- [ ] State the purpose of the call (or ask why they’re calling).
- [ ] Ask one question at a time.
- [ ] Provide clear options (“Yes/No,” “A or B”).
- [ ] Mention any fees or requirements upfront.
- [ ] End with a concrete next step (appointment booked, info sent, etc.).

## Step 5: Test with a Beta Group (Before Going Live to Everyone)

Never launch your AI voice agent to all customers without a soft launch first. Pick a small, trusted group of customers or leads and tell them they’re helping you test a new system.

**Your 5‑day beta‑test plan:**

- **Day 1:** Call the agent yourself from different phones (cell, landline). Check audio quality and script accuracy.
- **Day 2:** Have 2–3 employees or family members role‑play as customers. Note where the conversation feels awkward.
- **Day 3:** Ask 5‑10 friendly customers if they’d be willing to test your “new after‑hours answering service.” Offer a small discount or thank‑you gift.
- **Day 4:** Review the call recordings and feedback. Tweak the script and call flow.
- **Day 5:** Run a final test with the same group to confirm improvements.

**What to listen for during testing:**
- Does the agent **interrupt** the caller?
- Does it **misunderstand** common phrases or accents?
- Does it **sound too pushy** or too passive?
- Does it **handle objections** gracefully?
- Does it **transfer to a human** smoothly when needed?

Beta testing reduces the risk of frustrating real customers and gives you confidence before the full launch.

## Step 6: Launch & Scale Gradually (The SMB Way)

Small businesses win by being agile. Start small, learn, and scale.

**Week 1:** Turn on the agent for **after‑hours calls only**. Monitor the dashboard to see how many calls it answers, how long conversations last, and what percentage result in a booked appointment or captured lead.

**Week 2:** Add **inbound call‑routing during business hours**. Set the agent to answer when you’re on another call or away from the desk. Let your team get used to having an AI helper.

**Week 3:** Launch a **small outbound campaign**. Upload 50–100 leads from your website and let the agent make follow‑up calls. Compare conversion rates to your manual calls.

**Week 4:** Review your **key metrics** and decide where to expand. Double the outbound campaign? Add a second phone number for a different service line? The data will tell you.

## Common AI Voice Agent Pitfalls for Small Businesses (and How to Avoid Them)

1. **Picking the wrong voice** – Using a robotic, default voice because it’s free.  
   *Fix:* Spend the extra $10–20/month on a premium human‑like voice. It pays for itself in higher engagement.

2. **Over‑complicating the script** – Trying to handle 50 different scenarios from day one.  
   *Fix:* Start with a script that covers 80% of calls. Add branches as you review real call recordings.

3. **Skipping compliance** – Forgetting about Do‑Not‑Call lists or required disclosures for outbound calls.  
   *Fix:* Choose a platform that includes compliance tools (like VoxReach’s built‑in DNC scrubbing).

4. **Setting and forgetting** – Not reviewing analytics or updating scripts.  
   *Fix:* Block 30 minutes every Friday to check your dashboard and make one small improvement.

5. **Expecting instant perfection** – Getting frustrated when the agent isn’t flawless on day one.  
   *Fix:** Treat your AI agent like a new hire. Train it, give it feedback, and let it learn over time.
