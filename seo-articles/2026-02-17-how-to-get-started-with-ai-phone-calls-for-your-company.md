## Step 5: Design Your Call Flows and Scripts (The Conversation Blueprint)

The script is the heart of your AI phone calls. A well‑designed script feels natural, achieves your goal, and leaves the caller satisfied.

### Call Flow Design Principles

1. **Keep it concise** – Get to the point within 10–15 seconds.
2. **Sound human** – Use contractions (“I’m” not “I am”), natural pauses, and varied sentence lengths.
3. **Handle objections** – Prepare responses for common pushbacks.
4. **End with clarity** – Make the next step obvious (schedule, transfer, follow‑up).

### Script Template: Inbound Support Call

```
AI: “Thanks for calling [Company]. This is [AI Name], an AI assistant. How can I help you today?”
Caller: “I need help with my account.”
AI: “I’d be happy to help. To pull up your account, can you please provide your email address or account number?”
…
AI: “I’ve found your account. I can help you with [common issue]. Would you like me to guide you through the steps, or would you prefer to speak with a human agent?”
```

### Script Template: Outbound Sales Call

```
AI: “Hi, this is [AI Name] from [Company]. Is now a good time for a quick chat?”
[If yes]
AI: “Great! I’m calling because you downloaded our [resource]. I wanted to see if you’d be interested in a free 15‑minute demo to see how we can help you [achieve result]. Would you like to schedule that?”
```

## Step 7: Train Your Team and Set Expectations (Change Management)

AI phone calls will change how your team works. Proper change management ensures a smooth transition.

### Training Checklist

- [ ] **Explain the “why”** – Share the goals and benefits with your team.
- [ ] **Demo the AI** – Let them experience the AI calls firsthand.
- [ ] **Define handoff protocols** – When should the AI escalate to a human? How?
- [ ] **Update workflows** – Adjust processes to include AI‑handled calls.
- [ ] **Gather feedback** – Create a channel for team input and suggestions.

**Pro tip:** Position the AI as a **teammate**, not a replacement. “The AI will handle the repetitive calls so you can focus on the conversations that require your expertise.”

## Step 8: Pilot and Iterate (The Safe Rollout)

Never launch AI phone calls to 100% of your calls on day one. Start with a controlled pilot.

### 4‑Week Pilot Plan

**Week 1 – Internal Testing**
- Route 0% of live calls.
- Have your team make test calls, identify issues.
- Adjust scripts and call flows.

**Week 2 – Small‑Scale Live**
- Route 10% of live calls to AI.
- Monitor performance closely.
- Collect caller feedback.

**Week 3 – Expand**
- Route 30% of calls to AI.
- Review metrics, optimize.
- Train team on escalations.

**Week 4 – Evaluate**
- Analyze pilot results against success metrics.
- Decide: proceed, adjust, or pause.

## Step 9: Scale and Optimize (Continuous Improvement)

Once your pilot is successful, it’s time to scale. But scaling isn’t just about increasing call volume—it’s about continuously improving performance.

### Optimization Loop

1. **Measure** – Track key metrics weekly: conversion rate, CSAT, cost per call.
2. **Analyze** – Look for patterns: Which scripts work best? Which times yield highest answer rates?
3. **Experiment** – A/B test different scripts, voices, call times.
4. **Implement** – Roll out winning variations.
5. **Repeat** – Make optimization a weekly habit.

**Scaling Checklist**:
- [ ] Increase call volume gradually (10% → 30% → 50% → 80%).
- [ ] Add more use cases (start with support, add sales, then operations).
- [ ] Expand integrations (CRM → calendar → help desk → payment).
- [ ] Train more team members on managing the AI.
