## Step 1: Define Your Use Case – What Do You Want Your AI Agent to Do?

You wouldn’t hire a new employee without a job description. The same goes for your AI voice agent. **Start by getting crystal clear on the agent’s role.** Ask yourself:

* **Is this an inbound or outbound agent?**
  * *Inbound* – Answers incoming calls (customer support, appointment scheduling, FAQ handling).
  * *Outbound* – Makes outgoing calls (sales prospecting, lead follow‑up, appointment reminders).
* **What’s the primary goal of the calls?**
  * Schedule appointments
  * Qualify leads
  * Answer common questions
  * Screen candidates
  * Collect payments or information
* **Who will the agent be talking to?**
  * Existing customers
  * Cold leads
  * Job applicants
  * Patients or clients

**Actionable Takeaway:** Write down a one‑sentence mission statement for your AI agent. Example: “Our AI agent will call warm leads from our website form to schedule a 15‑minute demo with our sales team.”

## Step 4: Design Your Conversation Flow (Script)

This is where the magic happens. Your conversation flow determines what your AI agent says, how it responds to questions, and what actions it takes.

### Key Elements of a Great AI Call Script

1. **Greeting** – A friendly, professional opening that states who’s calling and why.
2. **Permission** – Ask if it’s a good time to talk (for outbound calls).
3. **Purpose** – Clearly explain the reason for the call in one sentence.
4. **Questions** – Ask open‑ended questions to engage the caller.
5. **Objection handling** – Prepare responses for common pushbacks (“I’m not interested,” “Call me back later,” “Send me an email”).
6. **Call‑to‑action** – What do you want the caller to do? (Schedule, qualify, transfer, etc.)
7. **Closing** – Thank them and confirm next steps.

### Example Script: Outbound Sales Call

```
AI Agent: “Hi, this is [Agent Name] calling from [Your Company]. Is now a good time for a quick chat?”
[If yes]
AI Agent: “Great! I’m calling because you downloaded our guide on [topic]. I wanted to see if you’d be interested in a free 15‑minute demo to see how our solution can help you [achieve result].”
[If no]
AI Agent: “No problem. What’s a better time to call back?”
…
```

## Step 5: Configure Your Agent’s Voice and Personality

The voice you choose can make or break your caller’s experience. You want a voice that sounds **natural, confident, and appropriate for your industry**.

### Voice Selection Checklist

- [ ] **Gender** – Male, female, or neutral.
- [ ] **Accent** – North American, British, Australian, etc.
- [ ] **Tone** – Friendly, professional, authoritative, empathetic.
- [ ] **Pacing** – Not too fast, not too slow (aim for 150–160 words per minute).
- [ ] **Emotion** – Some platforms let you add subtle emotion (enthusiasm, calmness, urgency).

In VoxReach, you can preview dozens of studio‑recorded voices and pick the one that best matches your brand. **Test multiple voices** with your script before making a final decision.

### Setting Agent Personality

Beyond the voice, you can define your agent’s **personality traits**:

* **Formality level** – Casual (“Hey there”) vs. professional (“Good afternoon”).
* **Verbosity** – Concise vs. detailed explanations.
* **Empathy** – How much it acknowledges the caller’s situation.

A well‑configured personality makes the agent feel more human and builds rapport with callers.

## Step 6: Test Your Agent Before Going Live

Never launch an AI voice agent without thorough testing. A single bad call can damage your brand.

### Pre‑Launch Testing Checklist

- [ ] **Dry‑run the conversation** – Call the agent yourself and go through every possible path.
- [ ] **Test with colleagues** – Have team members call and try to “break” the agent.
- [ ] **Check audio quality** – Is the voice clear? Are there any background noises or glitches?
- [ ] **Verify call transfers** – If the agent escalates to a human, does the transfer work smoothly?
- [ ] **Review transcripts** – Are the speech‑to‑text transcriptions accurate?
- [ ] **Measure latency** – Does the agent respond within 1–2 seconds? Longer pauses feel unnatural.

**VoxReach’s built‑in testing suite** lets you simulate calls, review transcripts, and see exactly where the conversation went right or wrong. Run at least 10–20 test calls before going live.

## Step 7: Launch and Monitor Your Campaign

You’re ready to go live! Launch your campaign and start taking or making real calls.

### Launch Day Checklist

- [ ] **Start with a small batch** – If outbound, send 50–100 calls first to gauge performance.
- [ ] **Monitor in real‑time** – Watch the live call dashboard for any issues.
- [ ] **Be available for escalations** – Have a human ready to take over if the agent gets stuck.
- [ ] **Collect feedback** – Ask early callers for their honest opinion about the AI agent.

### Key Metrics to Track

* **Answer rate** – What percentage of outbound calls are picked up?
* **Conversion rate** – How many calls achieve the desired outcome (schedule, qualification, etc.)?
* **Average call duration** – Shorter calls may indicate the agent is rushing; longer calls may mean it’s getting stuck.
* **Caller sentiment** – Are callers happy, neutral, or frustrated?
* **Cost per conversion** – How much are you spending per successful call?
